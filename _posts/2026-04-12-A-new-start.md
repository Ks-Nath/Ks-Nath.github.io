# Novum Initium

<h3>WHY DID I MAKE THIS ?</h3>

Well, I wanted to be more disciplined. I no longer want to be a bitch to the attention seeking heat missiles sent by corporate fucks.

> "Good systems turn hard habits into default behavior."

I think I just had a small existential “what am I doing with my life” moment.

Somewhere along the way, I stopped being… me. The version of me that actually did things instead of just thinking about doing them.

Right now, I’m juggling two startups, trying to learn medicine, and a hundred other things I’ve probably forgotten. The result? I overwhelm myself so much that I end up being weirdly unproductive. It’s like I’m addicted to the idea of working hard rather than actually doing the work. Peak delusion.

So I decided I want to change that. I want to get better—not in theory, not in my head—but in real life.

And weirdly enough, I think *WRITING* might be the answer.

Writing feels like a way to shut down the chaos in my head and actually talk to myself (in a non-concerning, hopefully healthy way 😂). It forces me to slow down, think clearly, and make sense of what’s going on instead of just spiraling.

More importantly, writing forces me to *CREATE* instead of endlessly *CONSUMING* content like a passive NPC. It gives me a creative outlet—something that’s mine. I can write about my thoughts, my systems, my ideas, or literally whatever the hell I feel like. No filters.

And no, this isn’t monetized. I genuinely don’t care if anyone reads this. This is just a space for me to think, build, and grow.

While thinking about all this, I fell into the rabbit hole of mental models and landed on one that actually makes sense to me: systems thinking.

Instead of relying on motivation (which clearly betrays me), I’m trying to build a system that makes productivity almost automatic—or at least less painful.

My setup? Nothing fancy.

Just **Google Calendar** and **Google Sheets**—my two weapons of choice.

Google Calendar handles my tasks and schedule. Simple, clean, no nonsense.
Google Sheets is where I’m building a personal dashboard—to track things, manage my startup, and honestly just get better at using Sheets and Excel (which, let’s be real, is an underrated superpower).

No complicated apps. No aesthetic productivity traps.
Just simple tools that work.

Let’s see if this version of me actually shows up.
